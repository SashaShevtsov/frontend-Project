Genres

  • Fantasy

    Fantasy is a genre of speculative fiction set in a fictional universe, often inspired by real world myth and folklore.

  • Action and Adventure

    Action and adventure books constantly have you on the edge of your seat with excitement, as your fave main character repeatedly finds themselves in high stakes situations.

  • Detective and Mystery

    The plot always revolves around a crime of sorts that must be solved—or foiled—by the protagonists.

  • Classics

    The classics have been around for decades, and were often groundbreaking stories at their publish time, but have continued to be impactful for generations, serving as the foundation for many popular works we read today.

  • Historical Fiction

    These books are based in a time period set in the past decades, often against the backdrop of significant (real) historical events.

  • Science Fiction

    Science fiction stories lean heavily on themes of technology and future science.

Metaphor
is a direct and vivid comparison between two things usually considered distinct or unrelated.
Hyperbole
is a remarkably exaggerated statement or idea meant to be taken figuratively rather than literally.
Climax
is one of the most important and necessary elements of a story’s plot, as all drama that has been developing over the course of the story reaches a breaking point when something or someone must change.
Lingo
language or vocabulary that is specific to a certain subject, group of people, or region; including slang and jargon.
Flashback
a moment in which the linear story is interrupted and launched to an event that occurred in the past.
